So now lets explain the different element of the frame data.


The hit level is if the move is high, mid or low.

The damage is how much damage the attack does in hit.

The start up frame is the speed of the move.

The block frame is the advantage or disadvantage if the opponent block.

The hit frame is the advantage or disadvantage if the oppenent get hit.

The counter hit frame is the advantage or disadvantage if the oppenent get counter hit.

The notes is if the move have special properties.


Negative on block

If you do a move who is -10 on block, that mean that you can't do anyting during 10 frames. On this case, your opponent can do a jab to punish you, but imagine if you do a move who is -15, your opponent can start a combo on punish.


Safe

If you do a move who is less than -10 frames on block or with enough pushback, you are safe, and can't be punish.


Positive on block

If you do a move who is +5 on block mean that your opponent can't do a lot of thing, cause if he do something who take more than 5 frames, he can take a jab from you cause the jab is 10 frames fast, 10-5=-5, jab-advantage=not a good idea to move.


Punish wiff

If your opponent whiff in case you duck is attack or he miss is attack, you can punish him with the appropriate move, in a lot of case, the recovery of the move is enough longer to launch him.


Speed

The game is turning in 60fps, so if you do a jab, the impact will take 1/6 second to occur.


Directional inputs

A sidestep is 6 frames, duck or standing is 1 frame, dashing and backdashing depend and your execution and the character.
